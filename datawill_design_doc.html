01 — Design Philosophy

Gravity without coldness.

DataWill sits at the intersection of legal infrastructure and personal legacy. The design must hold two things in tension: the authority of a financial institution and the warmth of a letter written to someone you love.

From Stripe: deep architectural surfaces, editorial typographic scale, structured information hierarchy, and the signal of trust through restraint. From Superlist: generous breathing room, serif warmth, human-centric copy framing, and the sense that a real person made this for you.

"Design for the moment someone opens this after losing a parent. It should feel like a calm, trusted guide — not a bank, not a chatbot, not a startup."

Trustworthy

Every surface signals security. Dark ink backgrounds, structured data tables, cryptographic honesty in copy.

Warm

Serif headlines. Cream surfaces. Generous spacing. The tactile quality of a well-made physical document.

Calm

No urgency patterns, no red warnings without reason, no dark UX. Every interaction is unhurried and clear.

Color usage rules

Backgrounds: Ink 900 for hero/dark sections; Mist 50 for page bg; White for cards.

Actions: Sage 600 for all primary CTAs. Never use Sage on dark backgrounds — use White or Sage light (#7AC4A0) instead.

Semantic: Slate for informational states; Gold for caution; Rose sparingly — only for destructive or irreversible actions.

Never: No pure #000 text on white. No bright saturated accent colors. No color-only meaning — always pair with text or icon.

03 — Typography

Two voices, one document.

Instrument Serif carries the emotional weight — headlines, quotes, key numbers. DM Sans does the structural work — body, labels, UI. Together they feel like a well-designed estate document, not a SaaS dashboard.

Typography rules

Serif for emotion: All H1/H2 headings, large numbers (stats), quotes, section titles.

Sans for structure: Body text, UI labels, navigation, buttons, form fields, data tables.

Line height: 1.05 for display, 1.3 for subheadings, 1.75 for body, 1.6 for UI text.

Never: No justified text. No letter-spacing on body text. No bold serif. Italic serif only for emphasis in headlines.

04 — Spacing & Layout

A 4px base grid.

All spacing is derived from a 4px base. Components use multiples of 4. Section-level whitespace uses multiples of 8. The max content width is 1100px; dashboard sidebars are 220px.

4px — tight inline gap (icon-to-label, badge-to-dot)
8px — component internal gap (input padding-y, button gap)
16px — card internal padding, list item separation
24px — card padding (standard), grid gap
40px — section separation (within a page view)
80px — major section separation (landing page blocks)
120px — hero vertical padding

Breakpoints

Mobile: 0–767px (single column)

Tablet: 768–1023px (2 column max)

Desktop: 1024px+ (full layout)

Grid system

12-column grid, 24px gutter

Dashboard: 220px sidebar + fluid main

Marketing: max-width 1100px, centered

06 — Motion & Interaction

Calm, unhurried motion.

DataWill deals with sensitive, sometimes emotionally heavy moments. Motion is deliberate and calm — never bouncy, never urgent-feeling. Every transition signals stability.

Duration tokens

instant 0ms
fast 120ms
base 200ms
slow 350ms
enter 500ms

Easing tokens

ease-out cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)
ease-in-out cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1)
spring cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1)

Interaction patterns

Page transitions: Fade-in at 500ms ease-out. No slides, no dramatic reveals. Content appears calmly.

Button hover: Background lightens/darkens 8% at 120ms. No scale transforms on primary buttons.

Cards: 2px translateY lift + shadow increase on hover, 200ms ease-out. Subtle, tactile.

Form focus: Sage ring expands from 0 to 3px over 200ms. Never jumpy.

Loading states: Skeleton screens (never spinners) using mist-2 with a slow shimmer. Signals stability.

Execution events: Rule status changes use a slow 500ms green fill animation — solemn, not celebratory.

10 — Voice & Tone

Honest, warm, precise.

DataWill's copy voice is drawn from Superlist's human-first tone, grounded in Stripe's technical precision. Never euphemistic about death — but never clinical either. Speak as a trusted advisor, not a legal document.

Write like this

"Your will is executed the moment your death is verified."

"Add a trusted contact — someone who can confirm your death and hold a recovery key."

"Your photos will go to your daughter. Exactly as you intended."

Never write like this

"In the event of your passing..." (euphemistic)

"Pursuant to the activation of the death verification protocol..." (cold/legal)

"Your assets are now at risk! Complete your will today!" (urgency dark pattern)
